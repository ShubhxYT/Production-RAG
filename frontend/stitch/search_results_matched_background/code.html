Quantum computing scalability remains one of the most significant hurdles in moving from NISQ (Noisy Intermediate-Scale Quantum) devices to fault-tolerant systems. Recent benchmarks suggest that superconducting qubits are leading in error-correction overhead reduction [1].

Specifically, the implementation of surface codes has demonstrated a threshold of approximately 10⁻³ for physical gate errors, which is critical for maintaining logical qubit coherence across expanding matrices [2]. However, the connectivity requirements for 2D surface codes pose a physical routing challenge as chip density increases [4].

Alternate architectures, such as Trapped-Ion systems, offer superior connectivity but face slower gate execution speeds. Scaling these requires modular photonic interconnects to link multiple trap zones, a technique currently being validated in specialized research laboratories [3].
